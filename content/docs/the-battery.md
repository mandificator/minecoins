# The Battery

Here's the twist that makes Promethium more than a mining coin.

When promethium decays at the surface, it **doesn't disappear** — it drains into **the Battery**, a reservoir living on Promethium Chain. Fitting, really: in the real world, promethium is literally used to make nuclear batteries.

## Where the charge comes from

Every miner who lets their surfaced promethium decay is, without meaning to, **charging the Battery**. Their loss becomes the Battery's gain.

```
   slow miners' decay ──> [ THE BATTERY ] ──> paid out as PROMETHIUM
                                              to everyone in the Battery Pool
```

## Where the charge goes

The Battery pays out its captured promethium to people who **stake $PROM in the Battery Pool**. Your share is proportional to how much you've staked. It's passive yield — powered entirely by the impatience of others.

## One catch (and it's thematic)

What the Battery pays you is **PROMETHIUM** — real, fresh, and it lands at the surface. Which means your yield **starts decaying too**. Collect it, then bridge it if you want to keep it. The loop never lets the element sit still.

## In short

- Don't bridge in time? You feed the Battery.
- Stake in the Battery Pool? You drink from it.
- Either way, no promethium is ever destroyed — it just keeps moving.
