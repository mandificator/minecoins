# FAQ

**Is this a real coin or a token?**
Both. **PROMETHIUM** is a real Proof-of-Work coin on Promethium Chain. **$PROM** is the same value mirrored on Solana, 1:1, backed by mined coins.

**Why does my promethium decay?**
Because promethium is unstable. Once it surfaces (after the 100-block haul), it has a **17.7h half-life**. The faster you bridge, the more of it you keep as $PROM.

**Do I lose everything if I'm slow?**
No. You always bridge, and the Bridge splits by time: what survived comes to you as **$PROM**, and only the decayed slice goes to the Battery. Slow = smaller slice kept, not zero.

**What happens to the decayed slice?**
It's not destroyed — it's captured into **the Battery** and paid out to Battery Pool stakers as $PROM. Your delay becomes someone's yield.

**Do I lose coins during the 100-block haul?**
No. The haul is completely safe. Decay only starts once promethium reaches the surface.

**What are the two staking pools?**
**Difficulty Pool** (stake $PROM -> mine up to 3x easier) and **Battery Pool** (stake $PROM -> earn $PROM yield from decay). Separate pools — use either or both.

**Can I bridge $PROM back into PROMETHIUM?**
No. The Bridge is one-way: PROMETHIUM -> $PROM. Bridge deliberately.

**Do I need special hardware?**
No. It's SHA-256, like Bitcoin. CPUs, GPUs, and Bitcoin ASICs all work.

**Is there a pre-mine?**
No. Fair launch, mined from block 1. The 21M $PROM is minted once on Solana and locked — released only as coins are mined and bridged.

**What's "agentic mining"?**
Driving the whole loop — mine, bridge, stake — from the command line through an agent like Claude, via a `skill.md`. Handy for bridging fast before decay takes a slice.

**What is x402?**
Pay-per-call payments. Bridge and staking actions cost 1 USDC each, in USDC on Solana — no accounts, no subscriptions.

**Max difficulty discount?**
**3x easier.** Same ceiling for everyone.
